Born in 1997 in Neuchâtel, Ulysse Loup is a Swiss composer, bassist and double bassist.

His parents being actors, he is very early confronted with artistic performance. He began his bass studies at the age of ten in Geneva.

He then follows the pre-professional training of the AMR (Association Musique impRovisées)- CPMDT (popular conservatory) directed by the saxophonist Ohad Talmor. At the same time, he completes a gymnasium maturity with honors at De Saussure College in the music section.

Then comes the professionalization, Ulysse Loup passes the entrance exam to the HKB (Bern University of the Arts) directed by trumpet player Tom Arthurs and is accepted in Stephan Rademacher’s class. There he follows a complete curriculum combining theory, practice of the instrument and composition. Among his teachers are such important jazz personalities as Django Bates, Ralph Alessi, Stefan Schultze, Colin Valon, Ronny Graupe, etc….

At the same time, he started his career as a composer-performer and toured all over Switzerland with different projects.

He has played at the Fête des Vignerons (Vevey), the Mehrspur (Zürich), the Alhembra (Geneva), the Mahogany Hall (Bern), the Cortona Jazz Festival (Italy), etc…

Today and after having scoured the Swiss scene as a side-man, Ulysse Loup concentrates on his own projects. He has founded a collective dedicated to free improvisation with flutist Ludmilla Mercier and double bassist Loïc Baillod and is preparing his first EP scheduled for January 2021 with pianist Daniel Hernandez and drummer Michael Cina.

He is also involved in cross-over performances with actor René Claude Emery.
